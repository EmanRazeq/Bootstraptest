MacBooks

The classics

Macbooks changed the world forever when they first started adapting. One classic Macs had started developing was the original Macintosh that was released in 1984. The Powerbook 100 was Apples first ever laptop that had a keyboard that made your palms rest on a trackball, but the disk drive wasn't that great. The price of this was $2,500. It had 2 to 8 megabytes of memory. a 9-inch monochrome liquid crystal display, with a 640 x 400 pixel resolution, and was known for its unique compact design of the trackball pointing device. It was very popular at the time and John Sculley who was the one that started the Powerbook and had made $1 million for marketing, leaving a sucess behind the Powerbook. Since the first Powerbook was a success, Sculley decided to continue with this product design but instead he extended the way it looks by adding different features making it even more popular and making $25 million used for marketing. Even though this was a success, Apple decided that they wanted help from the Company "Sony" because Apple did not have enough engineers to produce the number of successful products that people were buying. Sony had very little experience in trying to build these personalized computers, but after 13 months of giving the Powerbook 100 their top priority, it became the main focus in 1992. The battery lasted about 2 1/2-3 hours long and Apple's purpose in designing it the way they did was because they wanted to make it a more personal accessory as if you are carrying a wallet or briefcase with you.
